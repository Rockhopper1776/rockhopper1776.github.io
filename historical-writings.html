The Battle of Quiberon Bay

Posted July 8, 2026 at 6:05 PM CDT

This article examines the Battle of Quiberon Bay, one of the most consequential naval engagements of the French and Indian War and the wider Seven Years’ War. Fought on November 20, 1759, off the stormy coast of France, the battle pitted Admiral Edward Hawke’s Royal Navy against the French fleet under the Comte de Conflans. While Britain’s victories at Louisbourg, Quebec, and Fort Duquesne are often remembered as the turning points of the war in North America, Quiberon Bay ensured that France could no longer challenge British command of the sea, reinforce New France effectively, or carry out its planned invasion of Britain. The article places the battle within the broader context of imperial rivalry, naval blockade, French invasion plans, and Britain’s rise as the dominant maritime power of the eighteenth century.
